Abstract & Motivation

Food recognition traditionally stops at classification (e.g., "This is Pizza"). Dishcovery bridges the gap between visual data and culinary insights by implementing an "Inverse Cooking" system. It performs Visual Question Answering (VQA) to predict ingredients and generate cooking instructions from a single image.

Applications:

  • Dietary Tracking & Nutrition Analysis.
  • Recipe Retrieval from visual records.
  • Cultural Preservation of undocumented dishes.
Problem Statement

Given an input image I of a prepared food dish, the system must:

  1. Set Prediction: Predict an unordered set of ingredients S = {s1, s2, ..., sN}.
  2. Sequence Generation: Generate a sequence of cooking instructions R conditioned on both I and S.

Methodology: Inverse Cooking Architecture

Our approach decomposes recipe generation into two interdependent stages to handle the high intra-class variance in food images.

1. Visual Encoder (ResNet50) Extracts spatial feature maps (14x14x2048) from the input image. Pre-trained on ImageNet.
2. Ingredient Decoder (Transformer) Predicts ingredients as a set. Uses a specialized loss to optimize Intersection over Union (IoU) rather than order, handling variable ingredient counts naturally.
3. Recipe Decoder (Transformer) Generates the title and cooking steps. It employs co-attention to attend to both the visual features and the predicted ingredient embeddings simultaneously.
